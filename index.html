    Lecture format

    • Very interactive & engaged
      • Format is conversational
      • Negotiated understanding
      • An ongoing conversation between the presenter and one participant
    • The Presenter role
      • Will engage with participants throughout
      • Lecturer will shift the learner role randomly among participants throughout the conversation
    • The Learner role
      • In an active conversation with the presenter
      • You'll be asked to step through a specific path of reasoning
        • Follow that narrative through to confirm it fully captures something
        • The hard part will be setting aside competing intuitions for where to focus

    Lecture format

    • The Participant role
      • Pretend you are in the learner role
        • "Could I answer the question the lecturer just asked?"
          • If it turns out you couldn't have, raise your hand!
        • Take a note only when the learner takes a note
      • Expected to understand the entire context and conversation
        • Expected to voice all confusion or uncertainty along the way
          • We can't succeed at the goal of getting you through the material otherwise
        • Raise hand immediately for totally new/unfamiliar words or concepts
        • For accumulated confusion, raise hand before we move on from the topic at the latest
      • Will be cold called into the learner role
        • All questions by lecturer are directed at the current learner
      • Should not self select to answer questions posed by the presenter
        • Can self select to ask questions
      • Notetaking
        • Resist the urge to write something down before you fully understand it

    Lecture format

  • The Observer role
    • May choose to attend or not attend a lecture
      • But should not enter or exit mid lecture
    • Not obligated to keep up with the conversation
    • Will not be cold called into the learner role
    • Should limit questions to ones they're convinced they shar with others in the participant role
    • Take notes at your convenience
      • Best practices still apply, but since you will be suppressing certain questions, you may not be able to achieve "full understanding" prior to taking a note.
