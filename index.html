## Lecture format - Very interactive & engaged - Format is conversational - Negotiated understanding - An ongoing conversation between the presenter and one Participant - The Presenter role - Will engage with Participants throughout - Presenter will shift the Counterpart role randomly among Participants throughout the conversation - The Counterpart role - In an active conversation with the Presenter - You'll be asked to step through a specific path of reasoning - Follow that narrative through to confirm it fully captures something - The hard part will be setting aside competing intuitions for where to focus
## Lecture format - The Participant role - Pretend you are in the Counterpart role - "Could I answer the question the Presenter just asked?" - If it turns out you couldn't have, raise your hand! - Take a note only when the Counterpart takes a note - Expected to understand the entire context and conversation - Expected to voice all confusion or uncertainty along the way - We can't succeed at the goal of getting you through the material otherwise - Raise hand immediately for totally new/unfamiliar words or concepts - For accumulated confusion, raise hand before we move on from the topic at the latest - Will be cold called into the Counterpart role - All questions by Presenter are directed at the current Counterpart - Should not self select to answer questions posed by the Presenter - The question that comes soonest in content order takes precedence! - Notetaking - Resist the urge to write something down before you fully understand it
## Lecture format - The Observer role - May choose to attend or not attend a lecture - But should not enter or exit mid lecture - Not obligated to keep up with the conversation - Will not be cold called into the Counterpart role - Should limit questions to ones they're convinced they share with others in the Participant role - Take notes at your convenience - Best practices still apply, but since you will be suppressing certain questions, you may not be able to achieve "full understanding" prior to taking a note.
